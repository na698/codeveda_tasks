Phishing Awareness Training

Learn how to recognize and avoid phishing attacks

1. What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick you into giving away sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data. These attacks often come through emails, fake websites, or messages.

2. Recognizing Phishing Emails

  • Suspicious sender address (typos, unknown domains).
  • Urgent or threatening language.
  • Grammar and spelling mistakes.
  • Fake links (hover to see actual URL).
  • Unexpected attachments.

3. Spotting Fake Websites

  • Check for HTTPS & padlock, but be cautious of fake SSL.
  • Verify domain spelling (e.g., paypa1.com vs paypal.com).
  • Be cautious of poor design or pop-up logins.

4. Social Engineering Tactics

  • Impersonation: Pretending to be your boss or IT team.
  • Pretexting: Fake scenarios to gain trust.
  • Baiting: Free downloads or offers.
  • Quid pro quo: Fake tech support help in exchange for info.

5. Best Practices

  • Think before you click links or attachments.
  • Do not share personal info via email.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Report suspicious emails.
  • Keep your software updated.

6. Quick Quiz
